# 0.4 — Creature richness brainstorm (living creatures + the pet)

Companion to `TODO.md`'s `## 0.4 — THE LIVING CREATURES` section. A wishlist of **emergent** creature
features — everything short of human language / higher cognition; shared animal traits, communication, and
social behavior. Nothing here is scripted per-species: it all reuses the one substrate (scent/sound/charge/
shock fields), the value-based reinforcement cognition, the kinship graph, leadership, the genome, and
config over `if species==X`. Prune/pick when 0.4 starts; this is the idea bank.

## The keystone: COMMUNICATION EMERGES (signal = stimulus, meaning = learned response)
A creature emits a SIGNAL — deposit a scent, broadcast a call, strike a posture, touch, flash, vibrate —
which is just a STIMULUS placed in the world. Another creature PERCEIVES it (an omnidirectional range/scent
read, no vision needed) and its MEANING is whatever RESPONSE it has learned to associate (via `reinforce_cue`
— "that alarm call preceded a predator → flee"). So the whole communication layer is: (1) emit signal into
an existing field/broadcast, (2) perceive it, (3) learn its meaning from outcomes. What falls out for free:
- Alarm calls → stampede; contact calls → herd cohesion; mating calls → approach; food calls → gather.
- **Honest vs DECEPTIVE signals** — a false alarm that scatters rivals from food, mimicry (look/sound
  dangerous), a bluff threat display — all just signals whose learned reliability varies. Deception and
  skepticism EMERGE (a creature that's been fooled learns to discount that signaller).
- **Dialects / family signatures** — kin share a scent/call signature (already smell-based kin recognition);
  learned signal variants spread within a group like habits do.

## Communication methods (each = a substrate signal + emergent learned meaning)
- **Scent / pheromones** (reuse the scent field that rides the wind): territory marking (boundary scent),
  mate-attraction, **alarm pheromone** (fear scent), foraging **trail-laying** (ants → a scent trail others
  follow to food, evaporating over time — emergent shortest-path), individual/family scent signatures,
  dominance/status scent, distress.
- **Vocal / calls** (reuse `broadcast_call`): alarm, contact/cohesion, mating, distress, territorial, food-
  found, threat, submission. Different call types are different stimuli → different learned responses; volume/
  urgency scales the broadcast radius/intensity.
- **Body language / visual displays** (cheap posture states, NOT expensive vision): threat display (rear up /
  bare teeth / puff up → look bigger), submission (crouch/roll), courtship display (dance/plumage), warning
  coloration, tail/ear signals, freeze. Read by nearby creatures.
- **Touch**: grooming/allogrooming (social bonding + parasite removal), nuzzling, huddling (warmth + bond),
  play-contact.
- **Vibration / seismic** (reuse the shock field): ground-thumping / stomping → a seismic pulse others sense
  (elephant-style long-distance).
- **Electric** (reuse the charge field): electric fish sense/emit field distortions — navigation, prey
  detection, signalling in water/dark.
- **Bioluminescence / flash** (a light-signal channel): fireflies mate-signal, deep/dark signalling, startle
  flash.

## Social behavior
- **Dominance hierarchies** (extend leadership): pecking orders, alpha challenges, submission/appeasement,
  rank memory. **Reconciliation** after a conflict.
- **Cooperation:** pack/coordinated hunting (encircle prey), **mobbing** (gang up on a predator), **sentinel/
  lookout** duty (one watches while others feed → alarm call), **alloparenting** (help raise kin's young —
  reuse the kinship graph), cooperative nest/den defence.
- **Bonding & alliances:** grooming builds a bond/trust value; **reciprocity** (remember who helped/harmed —
  reuse the cue memory) → alliances, grudges, reputation. **Pair bonds** (kinship mate edges), courtship,
  mate-guarding, **mate choice / sexual selection** (choose partners by displayed traits → the trait spreads,
  like the bee↔flower selection).
- **Play** (juveniles play-fight/chase → learn skills + bond, low-stakes reinforcement).
- **Territory:** claim + scent-mark boundaries, escalate on intrusion, home-range fidelity.
- **Groups:** herd/flock/school coordination (have flocking) — synchronized flight, predator-dilution;
  **migration** (seasonal follow of resources/climate/temperature); nesting colonies.
- **Culture (thin):** habits/aversions/signal-meanings spread socially (have social learning) → local
  traditions/dialects that differ between groups.

## Shared animal traits (richness)
- **Senses** (mostly cheap, omnidirectional — smell primary per project rule): smell (scent field), hearing
  (calls/sound), touch, vibration (shock), electroreception (charge), thermoreception (temp field — sense a
  warm body/prey), taste; sight kept minimal/expensive. Sensory acuity is a heritable trait.
- **Rhythms & dormancy:** circadian (diurnal/nocturnal/crepuscular — have nocturnal), sleep, **hibernation/
  torpor** (cold → dormant, low metabolism) and **estivation** (heat/drought → dormant) — compose with the
  temperature field (and the compute-bubble LOD: a dormant animal is cheap).
- **Thermoregulation:** basking (have it), huddling for warmth, panting/shade-seeking in heat, migration.
- **Crypsis & mimicry:** camouflage (coloration matches terrain/season → lower detection), mimicry (resemble
  a dangerous species → predators that learned to avoid the model avoid the mimic too — emergent).
- **Predator/prey tactics:** startle, freeze, flee, fight, **thanatosis** (play dead), warning displays,
  school/herd for safety-in-numbers, distraction, ambush vs pursuit hunting.
- **Foraging strategies:** grazing/browsing, hunting, scavenging, filter-feeding, **caching/hoarding** (bury/
  store food for later → a stored-food resource; forgotten caches sprout plants — emergent dispersal), leftover
  sharing.
- **Crude tool use** (no higher cognition): use a rock to crack a shell, a stick to reach food — a learned
  action with a prop, not planning.
- **Parental care & teaching:** nursing/feeding young, guarding, juveniles LEARN by imitation from parents
  (social learning exists) — skills pass down without language.
- **Health & parasites:** the microbiome (0.4 nutrient cycle), pathogens/disease (bacterial overgrowth →
  illness → behaviour change; transmissible → epidemics that thin dense populations — emergent), grooming
  removes parasites, sickness behaviour (withdraw, rest).
- **Individuality:** heritable **personality/temperament** (bold↔shy, aggressive↔docile, curious↔cautious —
  via the genome) shaping every behaviour above; **curiosity/exploration** (novelty-seeking), **habituation**
  (stop reacting to a repeated harmless stimulus — reuse learning), **emotional states** (fear, contentment,
  aggression, playfulness) that drive both behaviour AND which signals get emitted.

## THE PET / COMPANION (the trainable creature — a large animal, player = permanent Leader)
Built on the shared systems (leadership pin + operant conditioning + all the traits above), the pet is where
the player forms a real bond with a non-verbal creature. Ideas:
- **Bond / attachment grows with care** (feeding, protecting, play, time) — a trust/affection value. Neglect
  or mistreatment erodes it → fear/distrust (flinches, keeps distance, disobeys); kindness → confidence +
  loyalty. **The pet reflects your treatment** (Black & White's soul): a loved pet is bold and devoted, an
  abused one cowering or aggressive.
- **Training via operant conditioning** (reward/punish → `reinforce_cue`): teach behaviours it already has —
  come, stay, follow, guard, fetch, hunt-for-you, herd others, scare predators, forage/bring food. No trick
  list; you shape which learned cue→action it fires.
- **Communicates its NEEDS non-verbally** (via the animal signals above): whines/nuzzles when hungry/scared,
  brings you to something (food, danger, a discovery), paws for attention. The player LEARNS to read it (and
  it learns to read you) — a two-way non-verbal channel.
- **Expresses emotion** so its state is legible: tail-wag/play/bounce (happy), cower/whine (afraid), growl/
  bristle (aggressive), droop (sad/sick). The player reads mood without words.
- **Preferences & personality:** develops a favourite food / favourite spot / favourite behaviour via
  reinforcement; its heritable temperament (bold/timid) colours how it responds to training and the world.
- **Companion in the world:** defends the player, helps hunt/forage, herds livestock, marks/patrols territory,
  fetches, alerts you (alarm call) to danger — using the SAME cooperative/communication systems as wild
  animals.
- **Social with other creatures:** it has kinship/leadership too — it can lead a pack, bond with other pets,
  get jealous / seek your attention, imitate you or other animals (social learning).
- **Grows & ages with you** (the life cycle) — juvenile→adult→old, learns more over its life, and its
  eventual death carries real stakes because you raised it. (Optionally: it can have offspring you also raise.)

## How this composes (so 0.4 builds it emergently, not as scripted content)
- **One signal system** (emit-into-field/broadcast + perceive + learn-meaning) underlies ALL communication —
  build it once; every specific "alarm call / mating scent / threat display" is a config'd signal, its
  meaning a learned response. Deception, dialects, honest-signalling fall out.
- **Traits are heritable config** on the genome (personality, sensory acuity, diet, thermal tolerance) → they
  vary, mutate, and are selected — evolution over generations emerges (mate choice, camouflage, mimicry).
- **Emotions/needs drive behaviour AND signalling** — one internal-state model feeds the cognition (what to
  do) and the communication (what to broadcast).
- **The pet is not a special system** — it's a large animal with the player pinned as Leader + operant
  conditioning wired into the same reinforcement + all these shared traits. Its richness = the shared richness
  focused on one bonded individual.
